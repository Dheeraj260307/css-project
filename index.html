🏁 TYPES OF TYRES
MotoGP uses Michelin as the official tyre supplier. Three compounds are available per race weekend — teams select which tyres to use based on track temperature, weather, circuit layout, and race distance.
S
SOFT
Soft Compound
☀️ Dry Conditions
Maximum grip, shortest lifespan. Used in qualifying and short sprints. Generates heat rapidly — ideal for cooler tracks or early laps. Degrades quickly under aggressive riding or high temperatures.
Grip
Durability
Warm-up
Best at: Qatar, Jerez, Sachsenring
M
MEDIUM
Medium Compound
☀️ Dry Conditions
The balanced choice — solid grip combined with good durability. The most common race-day selection. Works across a wide range of temperatures and suits riders who manage tyre wear well throughout a grand prix distance.
Grip
Durability
Warm-up
Best at: Mugello, COTA, Phillip Island
H
HARD
Hard Compound
☀️ Dry / Hot Conditions
Built to last in punishing heat. Lower peak grip but extraordinary durability over a full race distance. Chosen at abrasive circuits or in extremely hot ambient temperatures. Takes longer to reach optimal operating temperature.
Grip
Durability
Warm-up
Best at: High-temp circuits, desert tracks
W
WET
Full Wet Compound
🌧️ Full Wet / Heavy Rain
Deep grooved tread channels standing water away from the contact patch. Offers maximum aquaplaning resistance but generates little heat in the dry. Mandatory when race control declares wet conditions. Only viable in consistent heavy rain.
Wet Grip
Dry Grip
Water Dispersion
Used at: Any circuit in heavy rain (e.g. wet Sachsenring)
I
INTERMEDIATE
Intermediate Compound
🌦️ Mixed / Damp Conditions
The bridge between wet and dry. Light tread pattern handles damp patches and residual water without overheating on dry sections. The riskiest call — used when the track is drying but full slicks aren't yet safe. Often the race-defining strategy choice.
Wet Grip
Dry Grip
Versatility
Used at: Changeable weather races, drying tracks
A
ASYMMETRIC
Asymmetric Compound
☀️ Directional Dry Conditions
A special construction with harder rubber on the more-stressed side and softer compound on the other. Designed for circuits that heavily favour one direction — like Sachsenring's left-heavy layout. Balances grip and durability where a standard symmetric tyre would wear unevenly.
Inner Edge Grip
Outer Edge Grip
Balance
Best at: Sachsenring, Austin (direction-biased tracks)
* Michelin supplies tyres for the MotoGP World Championship. Each team nominates tyre choices before each round based on circuit data and expected weather. Race direction can mandate wet or intermediate tyres for safety.
